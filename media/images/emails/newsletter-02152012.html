ScamBook Documentary: IRS Tax Refund Scams Explained

In a exclusive ScamBook documentary, we reveal how many fall victim to false IRS Tax Refund scams. Phishing attempts are sent out as links in emails from bogus IRS or tax preparation software employees.

Recipients are informed of one of the following: they are eligible for a tax refund of specified amount, there are errors with the return they filed, or their software needs to be updated to comply with tax laws.

Whichever the case is, the victim must proceed by clicking on the provided link which takes them to a website which is made to resembles the legitimate IRS website.

Victims are asked to provide social security numbers, credit card numbers, and other personal data which is later used for fraud and identity theft. Phony software notification emails contain downloads to viruses instead of upgrades.

Free Credit Report Scam: Exclusive Documentary

People today use the Internet to get a hold of their credit score. Searching free credit report websites there are many to choose from. Many of these sites are legitimate, but the scammers have found a way to trick curious consumers by creating websites that replicate the legit credit sites to get you to enter your personal information. Before you can catch your own mistake, the scammer has all the information they need in order to swindle money out of your accounts and turn your credit score upside down.
